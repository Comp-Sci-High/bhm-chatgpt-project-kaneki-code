ALBUM
DON'T SLEEP
Sleepy Hallow’s debut project, Don’t Sleep, introduces his laid-back yet menacing flow, backed by eerie, melodic beats from frequent collaborator Great John. The album showcases his signature Brooklyn drill sound with standout tracks like "Breakin Bad" and "I Get Luv".
Don't Sleep
Released in 2019, Don’t Sleep serves as Sleepy Hallow’s formal introduction to the Brooklyn drill scene. Partnering with producer Great John, the project blends haunting melodies with hard-hitting 808s, creating a distinct drill sound that stands out from traditional gritty street rap. Songs like "I Get Luv" highlight his ability to weave catchy hooks with raw street storytelling, while tracks like "Breakin Bad" showcase his effortless delivery and dark humor. The album's title is a direct challenge to listeners—urging them not to sleep on his talent.

ALBUM
STILL SLEEP
Sleepy Hallow’s debut studio album, Still Sleep?, refines his melodic drill sound with polished production and a more personal touch. The project features the breakout hit "2055", alongside introspective tracks like "Mi No Sabe" and high-energy bangers like "Equal".
Still Sleep
Released under Winners Circle Entertainment and RCA Records, Still Sleep? is Sleepy Hallow’s most polished project to date, blending moody, atmospheric production with introspective lyrics and infectious melodies. The album's breakout single, "2055", showcases his ability to craft futuristic, dreamy drill music that resonates beyond the streets. Tracks like "Mi No Sabe" delve into his personal struggles, while "Basketball Dreams (Intro)" and "Equal" maintain the gritty energy that fans expect. Throughout the album, Sleepy Hallow hones his craft, proving that Brooklyn drill can be both emotionally rich and club-ready.

ALBUM
BMW
Departing from his pure drill roots, Boy Meets World finds Sleepy Hallow experimenting with new sounds, incorporating soulful samples, introspective storytelling, and more varied production. Standouts like "A N X I E T Y" and "Pain Talk" highlight his growth as an artist.
Boy Meets World
With Boy Meets World, Sleepy Hallow takes a bold step beyond traditional drill, embracing a more expansive, melodic, and introspective sound. The project incorporates elements of soul, R&B, and alternative hip-hop, demonstrating his versatility as an artist. Tracks like "A N X I E T Y" and "Pain Talk" reveal deeper emotional layers, while songs such as "GBG" still bring the gritty energy that made him a star. By fusing heartfelt storytelling with evolved production, Sleepy cements himself as more than just a drill rapper—he’s an artist capable of growth and reinvention.

ALBUM
SFP
Sleepy for President cemented Sleepy Hallow as a major force in Brooklyn drill, featuring a refined mix of storytelling, melodic flows, and menacing beats. Hits like "Deep End Freestyle" and "Molly" helped him break into mainstream recognition.
Sleepy-President
Following his 2019 debut, Sleepy Hallow returned with Sleepy for President, an album that solidified his signature style—melodic drill rap infused with introspective storytelling. The project balances bouncy anthems with moody, cinematic production, often featuring his close collaborator Sheff G. Tracks like "Deep End Freestyle" became viral hits, thanks to their hypnotic melodies and introspective lyricism. Meanwhile, songs like "Molly" display his effortless ability to switch between braggadocious energy and street poetry. The title reflects his growing influence, as Sleepy positioned himself as a leader in Brooklyn drill.

ALBUM
B-K
The Black House is a hard-hitting EP where Sleepy Hallow doubles down on his Brooklyn drill roots. With dark, hypnotic beats and street-focused lyricism, tracks like "Somebody" and "Perc Talkin" showcase his signature laid-back yet menacing delivery.
Black House
Dropped in 2020, The Black House is a high-energy EP that stays true to Sleepy Hallow’s drill origins. The project features menacing production from Great John, heavy basslines, and eerie melodies that complement his effortless flow. Songs like "Somebody" capture the raw intensity of Brooklyn drill, while "Perc Talkin" delivers hypnotic rhythms that make it an instant banger. Though compact, the EP maintains a cinematic feel, immersing listeners in Sleepy’s world of street narratives, loyalty, and ambition. The Black House reinforces his status as a key figure in the Brooklyn drill movement.

ALBUM
READ AWAKE
Sleepy Hallow's Read This When You Wake Up. is a concise yet impactful EP that blends his signature melodic drill sound with introspective lyrics. Featuring tracks like "Pain Talk" and "Good Girls Ain’t No Fun", the project balances raw emotion with hypnotic beats.
Read Awake
Released in 2023, Read This When You Wake Up. serves as a reflective, late-night listen that showcases Sleepy Hallow’s evolution beyond drill. The EP leans into melancholic storytelling, capturing themes of pain, success, and relationships over moody, atmospheric production. Songs like "Pain Talk" highlight his struggles with fame and personal demons, while "Good Girls Ain’t No Fun" brings a smoother, more melodic approach. Despite its short length, the project solidifies Sleepy’s ability to balance vulnerability with street sensibility, making it a compelling listen.
